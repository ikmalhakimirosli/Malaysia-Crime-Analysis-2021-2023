### üîç Project Overview
This research project analyzes the evolution of crime trends and patterns in Malaysia across different states and categories from 2021 to 2023. Using data provided by the **Royal Malaysia Police (PDRM)**, our team identified regional hotspots and emerging patterns to derive insights for public safety and law enforcement strategies.

### üí° Key Insights
* **Property Crimes Dominance:**

    Property crimes account for approximately **79%** of total incidents reported.
  
* **Top Crime Category:**

    Motorcycle theft is the most frequent sub-category, with **487,977 incidents** recorded over the three-year period.
  
* **Geographic Hotspots:**

    Urbanized regions, specifically **Selangor** and **W.P. Kuala Lumpur**, consistently report the highest crime intensity.
  
* **Temporal Trends:**

    Crime rates showed a decline in 2022 (correlating with pandemic-related restrictions) followed by a resurgence in 2023 as economic activities normalized.
  
* **Assault Crimes:**

    While property crimes fluctuated significantly, assault crimes remained relatively stable across the period.
